| <p>Meta open-sources <strong>Perception Encoder Audiovisual (PE-AV)</strong>, the multimodal encoder that powers SAM Audio.</p><p>Audio, video, and text models usually live apart, which forces teams to stitch systems together. PE-AV starts from a simple idea: learn one shared representation that understands all three at once.</p><p>Meta builds PE-AV on its earlier Perception Encoder and extends it to audio. The model trains with contrastive learning, which pulls matching audio, video, and text closer in embedding space while pushing mismatches apart.</p><p>Meta scales this setup across <strong>ten</strong> modality and caption pairings and trains on <strong>~100M</strong> audio-video pairs with synthetic captions covering speech, music, and sound effects.</p><p>This design produces a single encoder that works across tasks without task-specific heads.</p><p><strong>Key features</strong></p><ul><li>Unified audio-video-text embeddings from one encoder</li><li>Ten contrastive objectives across modality and caption pairs</li><li>Synthetic captions at scale across multiple audio domains</li></ul> |
